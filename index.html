Dissertation

The Memory of Being Hidden: Crypto-Jewish Identity in the Twentieth-Century U.S.–Mexico Borderlands

Dissertation advisor: Mark Goldberg

Traces how descendants of Iberian and colonial conversos preserved, reinterpreted, and publicly articulated Jewish memory across the modern U.S.–Mexico borderlands. Drawing on oral histories and archival research in Texas, New Mexico, and California, the project connects twentieth-century identity formation to longer histories of Sephardic diaspora, Atlantic migration, colonial religious regulation, domestic practice, and communal memory.
